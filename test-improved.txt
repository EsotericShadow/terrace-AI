If your dog is considered aggressive in the City of Terrace, here are the specific regulations you must follow:

- **Declaration**: The Animal Control Officer (ACO) may declare and record your dog as an aggressive dog at their discretion.
- **Licensing**: You must obtain an aggressive dog license.
- **Confinement on Property**: Your dog must be confined to your residence or only within the rear or side yard in a secure enclosure.
- **Off Property**: When off your property, your dog must be on a secure leash held by a responsible person with sufficient ability to control the dog, or kept in a locked vehicle or enclosure.
- **Sale or Transfer**: You must inform the ACO within 7 days if your dog is sold or given away.
- **Incident Reporting**: You must inform the ACO immediately if your aggressive dog is at large or has bitten or attacked a person or another animal.
- **Removal from Record**: After a minimum of 18 months, you may apply for removal from the aggressive dog record if you can demonstrate measures like behavior training and that your dog no longer represents a risk.
- **Rabies Quarantine**: If your aggressive dog bites a person and is not immunized against rabies, it must be quarantined for 10 days under ACO observation at your cost.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Signage regulations in Terrace are governed by the Sign Bylaw No. 2102-2016. Here are the key points:

- **General Regulations**: The bylaw applies to all signs within the city, with specific rules for different zones and types of signs. It includes regulations on the erection, placement, alteration, maintenance, demolition, and removal of signs.

- **Permit Requirements**: A sign permit is required for most signs, except for certain types like banners, building identification signs, construction signs, garage sale signs, portable signs, and real estate signs. However, even these exempt signs have regulations regarding their number, size, location, and duration.

- **Application Process**: To apply for a sign permit, you can visit the Planning section of the City of Terrace website for an electronic fillable form, or contact the City of Terrace Public Works at 5003 Graham Ave or call 250-615-4022. If hiring a sign company, the property owner must sign the permit or provide a letter of authorization.

- **Signage Limits**: The amount and type of signage allowed depend on the property's zoning. Each property has a maximum total sign area, and each sign type has a maximum permitted size. For multi-tenant properties, the owner can allocate signage within the total allowed for the property.

- **Home-Based Businesses**: Signs for home-based businesses (Home Occupations) require a permit. Level 1 Home Occupations are not allowed signs, while Level 2 and 3 are limited to one sign on a wall, window, or fence, with size limits of 0.4 m² for Level 2 and 1.2 m² for Level 3.

- **Community Event Signs**: These are temporary and can be placed on private property or in the public right of way with a permit and a deposit. A Community Event Signboard is available on Keith Avenue across from the Visitors Info Centre.

- **Third-Party Advertising**: Businesses are not allowed to advertise on properties other than where the business is located.

- **Zoning Information**: To find out your property's zoning, you can use TerraMap at https://www.terrace.ca/terramap or call Development Services at 250-615-4022.

For more detailed information, you can refer to the Sign Bylaw No. 2102-2016 directly.
